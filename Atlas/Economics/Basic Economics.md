**2008: Maryland**
In the state of Maryland, arise a need for money, "tax the rich!", advised a fellow from the crowd. At this period in Maryland, there were about 8000 millionaires which summed up to a $106 mil more a year, everything was fine for a year but the next year the number of millionaires dropped to 6000 and the economy felt a drop of $200 mil that year. 
"People respond to rewards not intentions"
In this case, the mayor intended to reward the citizens but leaving the country was a bigger reward for the rich as they wouldn't have to pay taxes.
As the rich left the country, they took their companies with them, people lost jobs and the state was into crisis
LESSON : **DON'T ASK WHAT YOU WANT PEOPLE TO DO, ASK WHAT YOU ARE REWARDING  THEM TO DO**

**Every Choice Has a Cost :** 
Lets say you have $10, you can have a coffee, a burger or a movie ticket, but, you cannot have all of them since you just have $10. Every single choice means giving up something else, these are ==*trade offs*==.

**Economics is not about what you have its about what you do with what you have**
for eg. Venezuela has many oil reserves, Switzerland has nothing in the ground, they should be poor, but in reality Venezuela is poor and Switzerland is one of the richest countries in the world. 

Every dollar you spent, every hour you work, every person you hire, the dollar can only be used once, the question is not "Can I afford this?", it is "What am I giving up on for this?".
for eg. You thought of buying a $50 k BMW, amazing car, would surely make you feel like you have done it, but if you were to buy a $20 k Toyota Corolla and invest the remaining $30 k you are looking at $60 k in the next 10 years or maybe $250 k in 30 years, the fact is, that BMW didn't cost you $50 k it costed you $250 k that you could have made , thats a trade off.
